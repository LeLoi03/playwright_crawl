          <div class="uw-copy-text">
  <div class="uw-copy-text__wrapper ">
    <p>
Masters
candidate
Niki
Hasrati
and
Professor
Shai
Ben-David
have
received
the&nbsp;<a href="http://algorithmiclearningtheory.org/alt2023/accepted-papers/">best
paper
award</a>&nbsp;at&nbsp;<a href="http://algorithmiclearningtheory.org/alt2023/">ALT
2023</a>,
the
34<sup>th</sup>&nbsp;International
Conference
on
Algorithmic
Learning
Theory.
This
year,
the
annual
meeting
that
explores
the
theoretical
and
algorithmic
aspects
of
machine
learning
will
take
place
in
Singapore
from
February
20
to
23.
</p><p>
Their
paper,
<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.04357">On
Computable
Online
Learning</a>,
studies
online
learning
with
computable
learners.
It
analyzes
that
question
under
various
requirements
for
successful
learning.
The
paper
gives
a
necessary
and
sufficient
condition
for
optimal
computable
online
learning
and
shows
that
the&nbsp;<em>Littlestone
dimension</em>
a
combinatorial
parameter
that
characterizes
online
learnability
without
computability
requirements

no
longer
applies
when
the
learners
are
required
to
be
computable.
</p><p>
Read
more
on
the
Computer
Science
<a href="https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/news/niki-hasrati-shai-ben-david-receive-best-paper-award-alt-2023">
news
page</a>.&nbsp;</p>
  </div>
